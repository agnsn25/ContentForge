Absolutely, it does make sense to do GEO/LLMO for blog posts, and it is quickly becoming a necessary effort.

Here is a quick breakdown:

Is It Worth the Effort? Yes.

GEO/LLMO is less of an alternative to SEO and more of an essential extension of modern SEO.

Future-Proofing: Search traffic is shifting. Users are increasingly getting direct answers from AI Overviews (Google) and chatbots (ChatGPT, Gemini). Optimizing for these new interfaces is how you maintain visibility.

Double Duty: The tactics that make content LLMO-friendly (clear structure, direct answers, strong E-E-A-T) also happen to improve your content for traditional search rankings and featured snippets. You are essentially doing two optimizations for the price of one.

Combatting Zero-Click: When a user gets a direct answer from an AI, they don't click a link. LLMO's goal is to ensure your brand is the source the AI mentions, which builds brand awareness and authority even without the click.

Is There a Chance That Chatbots/LLMs Will Quote the Blog? Yes, a strong one.

LLMs and generative search engines are designed to be authoritative and therefore rely heavily on citing sources. Your blog posts are quoted when they exhibit these qualities:

Authority and Trust: LLMs highly favor content from sites with high Domain Authority and those that demonstrate strong E-E-A-T.

Extractability: Your blog posts are easily quoted if they contain:

Original Data/Statistics: LLMs love to cite unique research, facts, or statistics. This is one of the most reliable ways to get a citation.

Direct Answers: Content structured in a Q&A format (e.g., using a heading "What is LLMO?" followed immediately by a concise definition) is easy for an AI to lift and quote.

Structured Content: Clear use of headings, bullet points, and semantic HTML (Schema markup) tells the AI exactly what information to extract.

Recency: LLMs often prioritize recent, high-quality information, so regular updates to your blog content increase the likelihood of inclusion.
